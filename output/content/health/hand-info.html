Understanding Hand & Wrist Pain

Hand pain is a common ailment that can significantly impact daily life. It primarily stems from repetitive strain, degenerative conditions like arthritis, and acute traumatic injuries.

Primary Causes of Hand Pain

A proportional breakdown of the most common origins of hand and wrist issues.

Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI)

Gradual damage to muscles, tendons, and nerves from repeated motions and overuse, often linked to occupational tasks like typing or using tools.

Arthritis (OA & RA)

Joint inflammation and cartilage deterioration from "wear-and-tear" (Osteoarthritis) or autoimmune conditions (Rheumatoid Arthritis).

Traumatic Injuries

Acute damage such as fractures, sprains, or dislocations resulting from falls, impacts, or accidents.

Building Your Defense: Ergonomics & Exercise

Prevention is the most powerful strategy against hand and wrist pain. Proper ergonomics in your daily tasks and a consistent routine of targeted exercises can build resilience and ward off injury.
