What Causes Knee Pain?

Knee pain is a widespread issue stemming from a variety of sources. While traumatic injuries are a major factor, chronic overuse and degenerative "wear-and-tear" conditions also play a significant role in knee health.

Your Proactive Playbook for Prevention

The most effective strategy against knee pain is prevention. Simple, consistent habits can build powerful resilience against injury and chronic pain. Strengthening the muscles around the knee provides crucial stability and support.

Knee Stability All-Stars

Strong muscles act as shock absorbers and stabilizers for the knee. Focus on these four key groups.

Quadriceps (Front Thigh)

Crucial for knee extension and stabilization.

Hamstrings (Back Thigh)

Controls knee flexion and supports the ACL.

Glutes (Hips/Buttocks)

Prevents inward knee collapse and ensures proper alignment.

Calves

Supports the entire lower kinetic chain from the ground up.
