What is Valentine's Day?

Valentine's Day, also called Saint Valentine's Day, is an annual festival to celebrate romantic love, friendship, and admiration. Every year on February 14th, people celebrate this day by sending messages of love and affection to partners, family, and friends.

The Legend of St. Valentine

The history of Valentine's Day is shrouded in mystery, but its roots are often traced back to a Christian priest named Valentine who lived during the 3rd century in Rome.

üìú

A Story of Defiance and Love

According to one popular legend, Emperor Claudius II outlawed marriage for young men, believing that single men made better soldiers. Valentine, realizing the injustice of the decree, defied Claudius and continued to perform marriages for young lovers in secret. When Valentine's actions were discovered, Claudius ordered that he be put to death. Before his execution, he is said to have sent the first "valentine" greeting himself after he fell in love with the jailer's daughter. He allegedly signed the letter "From your Valentine," an expression that is still in use today.
