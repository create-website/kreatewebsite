An Alliance of Survival: The World of 1621

The 1621 feast was not a simple gathering of friends. It was a moment of fragile diplomacy between two groups facing existential threats, each using the other for their own survival.

The Wampanoag's Great Dying

A plague (1616-1619) introduced by European traders killed up to 90% of the coastal Indigenous population, leaving the Wampanoag confederacy vulnerable to their rivals.

The Colonists' First Winter

Arriving in winter 1620, the Plymouth colonists were ravaged by disease and starvation. By spring, only about half of the 102 passengers and crew were still alive.

Deconstructing the Feast: 1621 vs. Today

The modern Thanksgiving meal is a 20th-century invention. The original 3-day harvest celebration featured a very different menu, reflecting the seasonal, wild bounty of New England.

The 17th Century Menu

  • ●
    Protein: Venison (5 deer from Wampanoag), wildfowl (ducks, geese), and seafood (lobster, cod, clams).
  • ●
    Vegetables: Flint corn (as porridge), squash, beans, pumpkins.
  • ●

Forging a National Myth

For 200 years, the 1621 feast was a forgotten footnote. It was resurrected in the 19th century to create a unifying origin story for a nation divided by civil war.

1863: Lincoln's Proclamation

Amid the Civil War, President Abraham Lincoln, urged by editor Sarah Josepha Hale, declares Thanksgiving a national holiday to foster unity. The 1621 feast is adopted as its origin story.

1924: Commerce & Culture

The first Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade is held, cementing the holiday's link to the start of the Christmas shopping season and popular entertainment.

1941: A Date Set in Law

After confusion over the date, Congress passes a law, signed by FDR, permanently fixing Thanksgiving on the fourth Thursday of November.

A Day of Mourning

For many Indigenous peoples, Thanksgiving is a painful reminder of colonization. The myth of a peaceful feast erases the brutal history that followed.

King Philip's War (1675-76)

40%

Native Population Killed

The 50-year peace unraveled into a devastating war that ended Indigenous sovereignty in New England. Colonists declared "thanksgivings" for military victories.

National Day of Mourning

1970

A Counter-Narrative Begins

After his truthful speech was censored, Wampanoag leader Wamsutta (Frank) James established this annual protest to honor Indigenous ancestors and expose the Thanksgiving myth.

Understanding Thanksgiving requires acknowledging its full, complex, and often contradictory history.
