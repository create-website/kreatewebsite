The Modern Holiday

Date and Meaning

In the United States, **Thanksgiving Day** is celebrated annually on the fourth Thursday of November, marking the unofficial start of the winter holiday season. While its origins lie in a complex historical context dating back to the 17th century, the modern holiday centers on themes of **gratitude**, family gathering, and feasting. Families and friends travel great distances to share a traditional meal, typically featuring turkey, stuffing, and pumpkin pie. It serves as a reminder to pause and reflect on the blessings and bounty of the past year.

The 1621 First Feast

Pilgrims and Wampanoag

The historical event often cited as the first Thanksgiving occurred in **November 1621** in Plymouth, Massachusetts. The **Pilgrims**, who had suffered greatly during their first year in the New World, celebrated a successful harvest. They were joined by around 90 **Wampanoag** Native Americans, who had provided crucial agricultural and survival knowledge that allowed the colonists to endure. This feast was a three-day celebration. Historical accounts suggest the menu included venison (provided by the Wampanoag), various fowl (likely duck or goose, not turkey), corn, and possibly shellfish. This event, however, was not called "Thanksgiving" by the Pilgrims; they would have referred to it as a "Harvest Celebration."

Core Modern Traditions

Beyond the feast, Thanksgiving is defined by a few widespread cultural practices that bring communities and families together across the United States.

1. Macy's Parade

Held in New York City, this iconic parade features massive balloons, floats, and musical performances, marking the official public start of the day.

2. Presidential Pardon

A symbolic and lighthearted tradition where the U.S. President "pardons" a turkey, sparing it from becoming part of the holiday dinner.

3. Football Games

Professional and collegiate football games are televised throughout the day, providing traditional entertainment for families gathered together.

4. Giving Thanks

Before the meal, many families share a moment where each person expresses something they are genuinely thankful for.
