Happy Diwali!

Welcome to the celebration of Diwali, the magnificent Festival of Lights. It is a vibrant, joyous festival celebrated by millions around the world, symbolizing the spiritual victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

Customs & Traditions

Diwali is celebrated over five days, with each day having its own significance. Homes are cleaned and decorated with intricate 'rangoli' designs on floors, and rows of 'diyas' (oil lamps) and fairy lights illuminate the night. Families gather to share feasts of sweets and savory dishes, exchange gifts, and light fireworks.

The Spirit of Laxmi Puja

The third day is the most significant, marked by 'Laxmi Puja'. Families worship Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth and prosperity. It is believed that on this day, she bestows her blessings for a prosperous year ahead upon those who welcome her into their clean and brightly lit homes.
