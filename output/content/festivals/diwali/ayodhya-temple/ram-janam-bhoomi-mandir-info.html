A Journey Through Centuries

The path to the Ram Mandir's realization is a story that spans nearly 500 years. This timeline highlights the most pivotal moments in its long and complex history, from early disputes to its celebrated inauguration.

1528: The Disputed Structure

The Babri Masjid is built at the site, which has been traditionally identified by Hindus as the birthplace of Lord Ram, setting the stage for a centuries-long dispute.

1949: The Appearance

Idols of Ram Lalla are placed inside the structure. The site is subsequently declared disputed, and the gates are locked.

2019: The Landmark Verdict

India's Supreme Court awards the disputed land to a trust for the construction of a Ram temple, concluding the decades-long legal battle.

2024: The Consecration

The Pran Pratishtha ceremony marks the consecration of the new Ram Lalla idol, and the temple officially opens its doors to millions of devotees.

Blueprint of Faith

Designed in the traditional Nagara style, the temple is an architectural marvel built to last over a thousand years without using any iron or steel. Its structure is a testament to ancient Indian engineering and craftsmanship.
