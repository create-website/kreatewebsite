The Heart of the Celebration

Date and Meaning

**Mother's Day** is observed annually on the **second Sunday in May** in the U.S., Canada, and many other parts of the world. The holiday is a dedication to honoring the mother of the family, as well as **motherhood, maternal bonds**, and the influential role mothers play in society. Unlike holidays with ancient roots, the modern American Mother's Day was intended as a day of personal, heartfelt appreciation, rather than an expensive celebration. It's a time for acts of service, handwritten expressions of love, and making the mother feel cherished and appreciated, often centered around **brunch** and the giving of **flowers**.

The Founder Who Fought Commercialism

Anna Jarvis and the Anti-Holiday Campaign

The modern Mother's Day was established by **Anna Jarvis** in the early 20th century to honor her own mother, who had been a peace activist. She successfully campaigned for the holiday, and in **1914**, President Woodrow Wilson officially proclaimed it a national holiday. Jarvis envisioned it as a personal day for children to write handwritten letters expressing love and gratitude. Tragically, she spent the later years of her life **fiercely fighting the commercialization** of the holiday, resenting the greeting card, candy, and flower industries for profiting from what was meant to be a non-materialistic expression of devotion.

Core Traditions of Celebration

These traditions embody the spirit of service and gratitude that defines Mother's Day around the world.

1. Wearing Carnations

A tradition started by Anna Jarvis: **white carnations** honor a deceased mother, while **pink or red carnations** honor a living mother, symbolizing purity and devotion.

2. The Brunch/Breakfast

Cooking a meal for Mom (often breakfast in bed or brunch out) is the most common gesture, as it symbolizes giving her a day off from domestic duties.

3. Sentimental Cards

Whether bought or handmade, the card is vital. The emphasis remains on expressing heartfelt gratitude in a written, permanent form.

4. Pampering Gifts

Gifts often focus on relaxation and self-care, such as spa treatments, scented candles, comfort wear, or jewelry.
