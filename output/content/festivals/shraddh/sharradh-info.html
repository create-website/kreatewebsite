The Sacred Bond: Understanding Śrāddha

An infographic exploring the profound Hindu ritual of honoring ancestors, from its ancient philosophy to its modern practice.

The Why: A Foundation of Three Sacred Debts

Hindu philosophy teaches that every person is born with three fundamental obligations (*Ṛṇatraya*). The performance of Śrāddha is the primary way to fulfill the sacred debt owed to one's ancestors.

🙏

Deva-ṛṇa

The debt to the Gods for sustaining the cosmos, repaid through worship and ritual.

📜

Rṣi-ṛṇa

The debt to the Sages for sacred wisdom, repaid by studying scripture and living righteously.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Pitṛ-ṛṇa

The debt to the Ancestors for life itself, repaid by performing Śrāddha.

The Who: A Hierarchy of Duty

Traditionally, the duty to perform Śrāddha falls to the son, but scriptures provide a detailed hierarchy to ensure ancestors are never neglected. Modern interpretations are expanding the role of women.
