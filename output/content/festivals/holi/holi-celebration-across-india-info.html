A Journey Through India's Holi

While the spirit remains the same, the expression of Holi changes beautifully with geography. Here‚Äôs a glimpse into some of the most unique celebrations across the country.

Lathmar Holi

Barsana, Uttar Pradesh

A playful tradition where women chase men and playfully hit them with lathis (sticks), re-enacting a divine story. It's a week-long, high-energy spectacle.

Holla Mohalla

Anandpur Sahib, Punjab

A martial twist on Holi celebrated by Sikhs. The three-day festival showcases incredible displays of swordsmanship, horse-riding, and mock battles.

Basanta Utsav

Shantiniketan, West Bengal

A graceful and poetic celebration of spring. Students wear yellow, sing, and dance to the tunes of Rabindranath Tagore, creating a serene cultural performance.

Royal Holi

Udaipur, Rajasthan

Experience Holi with regal splendor. The celebration starts with a magnificent palace procession, led by the royal family, culminating in a grand Holika Dahan bonfire.

Shigmo Festival

Goa

Goa's vibrant spring festival spans two weeks, featuring traditional folk dances, elaborate parades, and colorful festivities, merging Holi with local traditions.

Manjal Kuli

Kerala

A unique and gentle form of Holi celebrated in temples. Instead of synthetic colors, people use natural turmeric water, giving the festival a fragrant, golden hue.
