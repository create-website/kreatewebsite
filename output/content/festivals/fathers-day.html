What is Father's Day?

Father's Day is a holiday honoring fatherhood and paternal bonds, as well as the influence of fathers in society. It is a day to express gratitude and appreciation for the love, support, and guidance that fathers and father figures provide.

The Origins of Father's Day

The modern concept of Father's Day began in the early 20th century in the United States. The driving force behind the holiday was a woman named Sonora Smart Dodd, who wanted to honor her own father.

üí°

A Daughter's Tribute

Sonora Smart Dodd's father, William Jackson Smart, was a Civil War veteran and a single parent who raised six children on his own. After hearing a sermon about the newly recognized Mother's Day, Sonora felt strongly that fathers deserved similar recognition. She campaigned for a day to honor fathers, and the first city-wide Father's Day was celebrated in her home state of Washington on June 19, 1910. The holiday slowly gained popularity across the nation and was officially made a nationwide holiday in the U.S. by President Richard Nixon in 1972.
