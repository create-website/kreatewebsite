The Festival of Celestial Transition

This section introduces the astronomical foundation of Makar Sankranti. It explains how the festival marks a pivotal moment in the solar calendarâ€”the Sun's transition into Capricorn. This celestial event, known as *Uttarayana*, signifies the shift from darkness to light and serves as the cosmic backdrop for the rich tapestry of myths and cultural celebrations you can explore in the following sections.

Makar Sankranti is unique among Hindu festivals as it follows the solar cycle, occurring when the Sun enters the zodiac sign of Capricorn (*Makara*). This marks the start of *Uttarayana*, a six-month period considered highly auspicious. It symbolizes a cosmic journey from darkness to light, winter to spring, and is believed to be the "day of the gods," when the gates to the celestial realm are open.
