The Core Meaning of Spring Festival

A Festival of Renewal and Family

The **Chinese New Year**, or **Spring Festival (Chūnjié)**, is the most important holiday in China and East Asia. It marks the transition from winter to spring and is a time for honoring ancestors and uniting families. The holiday lasts for 15 days, culminating in the **Lantern Festival**. The most crucial element is the **Reunion Dinner** on New Year's Eve, where family members, no matter how far away they live, strive to gather. Every tradition—from the extensive cleaning of the home to the wearing of red clothes—is focused on **sweeping away bad luck** from the past year and **welcoming good fortune** (Fú) and prosperity for the new one.

The Lunar Calendar and Chinese Zodiac

Determining the Date and Sign

The date of the Chinese New Year is determined by the **Lunarsolar Calendar**, falling on the new moon between January 21st and February 20th. It marks the start of the year for one of the 12 animals of the **Chinese Zodiac**. The Zodiac operates on a 12-year cycle, with each year associated with an animal (Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, Pig). It's believed that the animal sign of one's birth year influences personality and destiny. The celebration is often named for the animal of the incoming year (e.g., Year of the Dragon), which governs the theme and many decorations for the entire festival period.

The Essential Family Traditions

These traditions, spanning 15 days, are mandatory for ensuring good luck and prosperity in the new year.

1. Grand Cleaning (Sǎochén)

The entire house is meticulously cleaned *before* New Year's Day to sweep away bad luck and make room for incoming good fortune. No cleaning is allowed on New Year's Day itself.

2. Reunion Dinner (Tuányuán Fàn)

The most important meal of the year, held on New Year's Eve. Dishes like dumplings (Jiǎozi) and spring rolls (Chūnjuǎn) symbolize wealth and prosperity.

3. Red Envelopes (Hóngbāo)

Money is given in red envelopes from married couples/elders to children/unmarried juniors. The red color wards off evil spirits, and the money symbolizes the passing of fortune.

4. Lion Dances & Firecrackers

Loud firecrackers and vibrant lion/dragon dances are used to scare away the mythical beast Nian and any lingering bad luck, purifying the atmosphere for the new year.
