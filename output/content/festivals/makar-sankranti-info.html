Myths That Shape the Festival

The rituals of Makar Sankranti are rooted in epic stories that give profound meaning to its timing and traditions, transforming an astronomical event into a landscape of divine action.

Bhishma's Passage

The great warrior Bhishma waits on a bed of arrows for the auspicious start of *Uttarayana* to depart his body, linking the festival to liberation (*moksha*).

The River's Descent

King Bhagiratha's penance brings the sacred river Ganga to earth to purify his ancestors' souls, establishing the sanctity of the Ganga Sagar pilgrimage.

Celestial Reconciliation

The Sun God, Surya, visits his son Shani, providing a divine precedent for mending relationships and fostering goodwill during the festival.
