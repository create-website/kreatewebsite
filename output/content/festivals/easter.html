Easter

A Celebration of Resurrection, Hope, and New Life

What is Easter?

Easter is the most important and oldest festival of the Christian Church, celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead. This event is the cornerstone of Christian faith, symbolizing victory over sin and death and the promise of eternal life.

Festival Timing

Easter is a moveable feast, meaning it is not fixed in relation to the civil calendar. It is celebrated on the first Sunday after the full Moon following the spring equinox.

A Message of New Life

The festival is strongly associated with the arrival of spring, and it carries a universal theme of rebirth, renewal, and hope for the future.

The Resurrection Story

The story of Easter is the climax of the Christian Passion narrative. After Jesus was crucified on Good Friday, his body was laid in a tomb, which was sealed with a large stone.

The Empty Tomb

According to the New Testament, on the morning of the third day (Sunday), Mary Magdalene and other women went to the tomb to anoint Jesus' body. They found the stone rolled away and the tomb empty. An angel appeared and announced that Jesus had risen from the dead. Later, Jesus appeared to his disciples, proving his victory over death. This miraculous event is the foundation of Christian hope, signifying that faith in Christ leads to salvation and eternal life.
