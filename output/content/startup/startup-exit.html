      Universal Key Considerations

      Regardless of the path you choose, careful planning is essential for a successful exit.

      Market Timing

      The state of the economy and public markets can significantly impact valuation and the viability of certain exits, especially IPOs.

      Financial Health

      Clean, audited financials are non-negotiable. Strong, predictable revenue and profitability dramatically increase your options and valuation.

      Legal & IP Preparedness

      Ensure your corporate structure, contracts, and intellectual property portfolio are well-documented and secure to withstand due diligence.

      Management Team Alignment

      All key stakeholders, including the management team and board, should be aligned on the desired exit strategy and timing.

      Investor Expectations

      Your investors have their own timelines and return expectations. Maintain open communication to manage these expectations effectively.

      Post-Exit Role

      Consider your own future. Do you want to stay on after an acquisition? An exit is a personal transition as much as a business one.
