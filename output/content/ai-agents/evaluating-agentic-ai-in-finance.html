Task Success Metrics

These metrics measure the agent's ability to complete its assigned objectives, evaluating the quality and correctness of its final output.

  • Goal Completion Rate: % of tasks successfully completed.
  • Accuracy/Precision: Closeness of the output to the correct value.
  • Task-specific Quality Score: Custom score based on desired outcomes.

The Agent Evaluation Lifecycle

Evaluating an agentic system is not a one-time task, but a continuous lifecycle. Click on each stage to understand its purpose and key activities.

1. Define Goals & Metrics

Establish clear business objectives and corresponding success metrics.

2. Offline Evaluation

Test on historical data and simulated environments.

3. Human-in-the-Loop

Review agent decisions with human experts before execution.

4. Staged Deployment

Gradually roll out the agent, starting with shadow mode or A/B testing.

5. Continuous Monitoring

Actively monitor performance, safety, and model drift in production.

1. Define Goals & Metrics

Before any development or evaluation begins, it's critical to define what success looks like. This stage involves deep collaboration between business stakeholders and technical teams to translate high-level business goals (e.g., "increase portfolio alpha") into specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) metrics for the AI agent. This ensures that the evaluation framework is perfectly aligned with business value and that everyone agrees on the criteria for success.
