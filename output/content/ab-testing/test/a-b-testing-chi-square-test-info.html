Making Sense of A/B Testing

An infographic on using the Chi-Squared test to find statistically significant results and make data-driven decisions.

What is A/B Testing?

It's a method of comparing two versions of a webpage, app, or email against each other to determine which one performs better. You show two variants (A and B) to similar visitors at the same time. The one that achieves a better conversion rate wins.

The Big Question: Is The Winner Real?

Variation B might get more conversions, but how do we know it wasn't just random luck? This is where statistical significance comes in. We use a statistical test to calculate the probability (the p-value) that the result was due to chance.

Using the Chi-Squared (œá¬≤) Test

The Chi-Squared test is perfect for A/B testing conversions. It compares the observed results (what actually happened) to the results we would expect if there were no real difference between the variations. A large difference between observed and expected results suggests the outcome wasn't random.

5.38

Interpreting the P-value

We typically set a "confidence level" before the test, usually 95%. This means we accept a 5% (or 0.05) chance of being wrong. Since our p-value (0.02) is less than 0.05, we can be confident the result is real. We can declare Variation B the winner!
